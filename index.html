about

I am a fourth year Biomedical Engineering student at the University of Waterloo. I am interested in the intersection of artificial and natural intelligence, particularly in decision making, neural plasticity, and learning frameworks.

I am a visiting undergraduate researcher at the Kempner Institute at Harvard University with Bernardo Sabatini, where I am collaborating with the Research & Engineering Team project developing modular computational models of the mouse brain.

Beyond research, I'm a director of WATOLINK, the University of Waterloo's neurotechnology student design team. Our mission is to provide hands-on learning and research opportunities for students developing brain-computer interfaces using EEG signal analysis and machine learning.
