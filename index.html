about me

I am a fourth year Biomedical Engineering student at the University of Waterloo with an option in Artificial Intelligence. I am interested in the intersection of artificial and natural intelligence, in decision making frameworks.

I am a visiting undergraduate researcher at the Kempner Institute at Harvard University in Bernardo Sabatini's lab, where I am collaborating on a Research & Engineering Team project, contributing to the development of modular computational models of the mouse brain.

Adjacent to research, I'm a director of WATOLINK, the University of Waterloo's neurotechnology student design team. Our team offers students an opportunity to develop applications involving action classification via EEG signal analysis and inference, aimed at creating brain-controlled solutions.
